Recent Work

KNOWLEDGE BASE IDE 621

Made by V. Soboleva
is a knowledge base devoted to three major learning theories: behaviorism, cognitivism, and social learning theory. It does not represent any of the IDD&E phases, as it is a more theoretical project. It was my first website I made on my own, and I am proud of that!

Open Project

Improving First-Year International Students’ Money Management Skills and Knowledge IDE 631

Made by W. Alkeheli, M. Montenegro, R. Pettit, V.Soboleva
This project describes a money management problem that undergraduate international students face and provides a prototype of an instructional solution for such a problem and represents the whole IDD&E process. It was my first experience working with the IDD&E model, and, therefore, a little challenging at first.

Open Project

Microsoft Teams for Online Staff Workshops IDE 611

Made by H. A. Afzal, P. Gavenda, V. Soboleva
This project describes an online-learning technology need faced by individuals within the Syracuse University Talent Management team, and campus partners and provides a prototype of an instructional design solution in a form of training. It presents the design & development stages of the IDD&E process. Being an enthusiastic person about technology, I found this project one of the most interesting ones.

Open Project

STEAM and games video IDE 552

Made by V. Soboleva
This project is a short video explaining how games can be integrated into STEAM courses at the K-12 level. A short quiz at the end is integrated into the video. Depending on the person's answers the video can move to the answer explanation section. It does not belong to any stage of the IDD&E process but remains one of the interesting projects I have ever had.

Open Project

Effective assessment strategies IDE

Made by V. Soboleva
This project presents an prototype of instructional design solution to the problem of lack of skills in students' assessment among professors in post-Soviet countries. This is a course that explains techniques to effectively assess student performance.
